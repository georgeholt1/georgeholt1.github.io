Overview

I am currently a physics PhD researcher in the Strathclyde Intense Laser Interaction Studies (SILIS) group at the University of Strathclyde in the UK. My work involves:

  • Developing and deploying research software and performing simulations on supercomputers;
  • Analysing and visualising simulated and experimental data;
  • Formulating and testing mathematical models to describe phenomena observed in simulations and experiment;
  • Communicating my work and findings with collaborators and the wider research community by publishing papers and giving talks at international conferences;
  • Designing, delivering and administrating undergraduate and master's-level taught courses as well as supervising project students.

Outside of my PhD, I enjoy learning about machine learning concepts and applying these methods to interesting problems.

I am also hugely interested in all varieties of data visualisation. A portfolio of some of my visualisations can be found here.

I use this site to post about projects I'm involved in, topics I find interesting and solutions to problems I've found that others might find useful. My posts are collected by year here.

Please feel free to reach out via any of the contact methods listed on the contact page.
